Calacatta Viola Marble

Timeless Luxury, Bold Elegance

Calacatta Viola is a rare and luxurious marble sourced from the Apuan Alps in Italy, an area globally recognized for producing high-quality natural stone. This marble is composed primarily of calcite, which gives it a smooth texture and soft luminosity, while the deep burgundy and violet veining comes from mineral traces like iron and manganese. These organic patterns make each slab entirely unique, offering designers the chance to create truly one-of-a-kind spaces.

Calacatta Viola is especially prized for its bold aesthetic and is often chosen for dramatic interior applications such as countertops, statement wall cladding, artistic furniture, and elegant fireplaces. Its ability to complement both traditional and contemporary styles adds to its versatility and appeal. Whether honed for a softer, matte appearance or polished to highlight its vivid contrast and depth, this marble brings a sense of refinement and drama to any space. It is often used in bookmatched installations, where mirrored veining creates a symmetrical and visually captivating effect.

Sleek Terrazzo Countertop

Modern Aesthetic with Lasting Appeal

Terrazzo countertops combine durability with artistic expression, offering a contemporary surface option that stands up well to daily use in kitchens, bathrooms, and other high-traffic areas. Created by embedding fragments of marble, quartz, granite, and glass into a cement or resin base, terrazzo is both visually dynamic and structurally resilient. Each surface is unique, featuring a mosaic-like pattern that can be subtle or bold depending on the size, color, and composition of the chips used.

The renewed interest in terrazzo is largely due to its sustainable nature and the flexibility it offers in design. Its eco-friendly profile appeals to those seeking greener building materials, while its customizable look allows it to integrate seamlessly into a wide variety of interior styles.

From clean, minimalist spaces to more vibrant, retro-inspired environments, terrazzo serves as both a functional surface and a statement piece. Its ability to bridge aesthetics and performance has made it a favorite among modern designers who value materials that offer both personality and practicality.

The material is also known for its low maintenance and longevity; when properly sealed, it resists stains, scratches, and moisture, making it ideal for both residential and commercial environments. Its thermal stability and compatibility with underfloor heating add further to its practical benefits. As a sustainable material, terrazzo often incorporates recycled content, which contributes to LEED certification and appeals to eco-conscious projects. This balance of tradition, innovation, and environmental responsibility continues to fuel terrazzo’s popularity in contemporary design.

Finding the Perfect Bathroom Tile:
A Guide to Style and Function

Choosing the right tile for your bathroom is about more than just aesthetics—it's about finding the perfect balance between beauty, durability, and practicality. With so many options available, from timeless marble to trendy terrazzo, selecting the right one can feel overwhelming.

Start by considering functionality. Bathrooms are high-moisture areas, so porcelain and ceramic tiles are excellent choices due to their water resistance and easy maintenance. If you love the look of natural stone, ensure it’s sealed to prevent staining.

Next, think about size and layout. Large-format tiles can make a small bathroom feel more spacious, while mosaics work beautifully in shower floors for added grip. Playing with patterns, such as herringbone or chevron, can bring a dynamic look to your space.

The Ultimate Guide to Choosing the Perfect Backsplash

A well-chosen backsplash can transform your kitchen or bathroom, adding both style and functionality to your space. More than just a decorative element, a backsplash protects your walls from splashes, stains, and heat—making it an essential design decision.

When selecting a backsplash, consider materials that suit your lifestyle. Classic ceramic and porcelain tiles offer durability and easy maintenance, while natural stone like marble or travertine brings a touch of luxury. For a contemporary feel, glass tiles or metallic finishes create a sleek, modern aesthetic.

Pattern and layout also play a significant role. Subway tiles remain a timeless choice, but experimenting with herringbone, chevron, or geometric designs can add a unique look. If you prefer a seamless look, slab backsplashes in quartz or terrazzo provide a clean and modern option.

Which Floor Type Should You Pick?

Flooring is one of the most important decisions in any renovation. From classic hardwood looks to terrazzo and natural stone, your selection impacts the overall feel of your home. It’s essential to choose a material that can withstand the activity of the space.

Porcelain tiles are an excellent option for high-traffic areas. They're extremely durable, water-resistant, and come in a wide variety of styles and finishes. For warmth, consider wood-look porcelain tiles or engineered wood in living areas.

Don't forget underfoot comfort and installation. Radiant heating under tiles is a great solution for cold floors in bathrooms or entryways. For open-concept layouts, choosing a consistent flooring material throughout creates visual continuity and flow.
